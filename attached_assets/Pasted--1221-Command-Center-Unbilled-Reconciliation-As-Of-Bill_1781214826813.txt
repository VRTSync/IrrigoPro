# #1221 — Command Center Unbilled Reconciliation (As-Of Billing-Month Cutoff)

## What & Why

The Command Center (`pages/customer-billing.tsx`) shows two headline numbers for
each customer — the left-list "Total" and the right-panel "Unbilled Work" badge —
but they are computed by two different endpoints using two different rules, so they
silently disagree. A real customer (Village of Five Parks Heritage) shows
$1,131.09 on the left and $1,211.09 on the right — an $80 gap caused by two
independent divergences:

1. The left Total uses a rolling 30-day window (a lower bound); the right badge is
   all-time. An approved billing sheet older than 30 days drops out of the left
   number only. This is a bug: aging unbilled work falls off the back.
2. The right badge counts approved-only items; the left Total counts approved +
   unapproved.

The page is an "unbilled work" surface scoped to the month you are billing for. The
correct date rule is not a rolling window and not all-time — it is an as-of cutoff:
everything with a completion/work date on or before the end of the selected billing
month, with no lower bound (open all the way back).

This is the important distinction from the bug in (1): the rolling window had a
lower bound and hid old work. The as-of cutoff has only an upper bound and hides
future (next-period) work, never old work. Anything aging and unbilled from any
prior month still appears as long as it is dated on or before the cutoff.

**Decision locked:**

- The list is scoped by an as-of cutoff = end of the selected billing month, applied
  to the completion/work date (WO `completedAt`; BS `workDate`; WCB `workDate`).
  Upper bound only; open start. No 30-day floor, no lower clamp.
- The selected billing month defaults to the previous calendar month relative to
  today, recomputed each day (on June 4 → May 2026; on July 10 → June 2026). The
  header states it plainly: "Billing for: May 2026."
- Within the selected month, both headline numbers equal approved + unapproved.
  Approved vs unapproved is a breakdown within that total. Invoicing still acts only
  on approved items.
- A null completion/work date never hides a record: undated unbilled items appear in
  every month's view, flagged "no work date — assign one."
- An "All open (no cutoff)" option remains available in the month picker, and
  Financial Pulse stays all-time, so total exposure is always one selection away.
- Both endpoints apply the same cutoff via one shared selector. The headline match
  (left Total === right badge) holds only because they receive an identical selected
  month and identical cutoff/null semantics.

## Done looks like

- Left list "Total" === right "Unbilled Work" badge for every customer, exactly,
  under the same selected billing month.
- Both = approved + unapproved for that month's cutoff (work/completion date ≤ end of
  selected month), no lower-bound reduction.
- Scenario (billing May 2026, today is June 4): work completed in June is excluded;
  approved/unapproved work completed in May or any earlier unbilled month is
  included; an unbilled item with no work date is included and flagged.
- The picker defaults to the previous calendar month; "All open" shows the full
  all-time unbilled total ($1,211.09 for Village of Five Parks Heritage).
- Approved/unapproved breakdown and WO/BS/WCB ticket-type tabs still render.
- "Select Items to Invoice" enables/selects on approved items only; stays disabled
  when only unapproved work exists.
- Every item behind a total is visible in the list below it, tagged Approved /
  Unapproved — no silent contributors. Undated rows are flagged.
- Per tab: `sum(visible row amounts) === tab subtotal`;
  `sum(tab subtotals) === headline total` (all under the selected month's cutoff).
- Unapproved rows are visible but their invoice checkbox is disabled.
- Cutoff + preview-vs-detail invariant tests pass.
- No existing visible billable becomes newly hidden other than future-dated
  (next-period) work, which is reachable by changing the selected month.
- Invoice construction paths are untouched.

## Out of scope

- Changing WCB creation status or adding a WCB approval step.
- Removing the `converted` gate from invoice-construction paths.
- Any QuickBooks sync behavior.
- Redesigning Financial Pulse math (Step 9 is verification-only).
- Data-quality handling of far-future-dated records (fat-fingered completion date in
  2027 scopes out of every near-term month); flag separately if needed.
- The WCB queue/tile double-count work specified in the WCB and Command Center
  Invisibility slice — coordinate the shared date-rule change; don't duplicate it.

## Steps

1. **Create `billing-unbilled-selectors.ts`** — New file at
   `artifacts/api-server/src/billing-unbilled-selectors.ts`. Define one canonical
   "current unbilled" selector accepting records plus an `asOfCutoff` argument (a
   `Date` = last instant of the selected billing month in the company's local
   timezone, computed from local date parts — not `toISOString` UTC; or `null` for
   the "All open" view).

   Cutoff rule (upper bound only, open start): include a record when its
   completion/work date `<= asOfCutoff` (WO `completedAt`; BS `workDate`; WCB
   `workDate`). When `asOfCutoff` is `null`, apply no date filter. Null work date:
   always include regardless of cutoff, and mark `undated: true` so the UI can flag
   it. There is no lower bound under any setting.

   Legacy normalization (before partitioning): BS `'approved'` →
   `'approved_passed_to_billing'` (schema-documented alias still present on records).

   Partition the cutoff-included records into `approved`
   (`status === 'approved_passed_to_billing' && !invoiceId`) and `unapproved` (status
   in the pending sets: WO `pending_manager_review` / `work_completed`; BS
   `pending_manager_review` / `completed` / `submitted`; WCB `submitted` /
   `pending_manager_review`).

   Exhaustiveness sentinel: for every record with `!invoiceId` whose normalized
   status falls outside both sets and outside the explicit excluded set (`pending`,
   `assigned`, `in_progress`, `draft`, `cancelled`, `billed`), emit
   `logger.warn('floating-billable', { type, id, status })` so a future new status
   fails fast rather than silently vanishing.

   Expose per-type arrays (each carrying `undated`), per-type subtotals,
   `approvedTotal`, `unapprovedTotal`, and `total` for the cutoff scope, plus an
   `allOpenTotal` (computed with `asOfCutoff = null`) for the safety-net view and
   Financial Pulse cross-check. Use the existing `safeAmount` coercion semantics from
   `routes.ts:6103-6111`. Status sets, the normalization map, and the cutoff/null rule
   are the single source of truth — not redefined inline in either route. Do not apply
   the `wetCheckStatus === 'converted'` gate here.

2. **Fix the preview endpoint (`/api/customers/billing-preview`)** — In `routes.ts`,
   resolve the selected billing month from the `selectedMonth` query param, defaulting
   to the previous calendar month relative to the server's current date when absent.
   Translate it to `asOfCutoff` = end of that month (local tz), or `null` when "All
   open" is selected. Source `approvedTotal`, `unapprovedTotal`, and `total` from the
   Step-1 selector with that cutoff. Delete the existing `*InRange` rolling-window
   logic (`routes.ts:6079-6096`, `:6114-6126`) — it is replaced by the selector's
   cutoff. Keep exposing `allOpenTotal` for the All-open view. Update the intra-payload
   drift guard at `routes.ts:6229-6243` to reconcile the cutoff-scoped fields.

3. **Fix the detail endpoint (`/api/customers/:id/billing`)** — In `routes.ts`, accept
   the same `selectedMonth` query param (same default = previous month) and build the
   same `asOfCutoff`. Source the unbilled partition from the Step-1 selector. Change
   `totalUnbilledAmount` (`routes.ts:6441-6444`) to `approvedTotal + unapprovedTotal`
   (the selector's cutoff-scoped `total`). Keep `unbilledWorkOrders` /
   `unbilledBillingSheets` / `unbilledWetCheckBillings` as the approved (invoice-able)
   arrays. Add parallel `pendingApprovalWorkOrders` / `pendingApprovalBillingSheets` /
   `pendingApprovalWetCheckBillings` for the unapproved bucket. The frontend must pass
   the same selected month to this endpoint as to the preview, or the two headlines
   will diverge.

4. **Fix the left-list display in `customer-billing.tsx`** — Per-customer card "Total"
   (`customer-billing.tsx:1036-1038`): use the cutoff-scoped total. Header summary
   (`customer-billing.tsx:814-816`): base Approved / Unapproved / Total on the
   cutoff-scoped fields. Sort (`customer-billing.tsx:725-726`): sort by the same total
   so list order matches the displayed number. List-inclusion filter
   (`customer-billing.tsx:715`, `:595-598`) gates on the cutoff-scoped total > 0 (with
   the All-open option using `allOpenTotal`).

5. **Add the billing-month picker + label** — In `customer-billing.tsx`, add a month
   selector that drives `selectedMonth` for both queries. It defaults to the previous
   calendar month (recomputed from today), offers prior months and an "All open (no
   cutoff)" entry, and the header reads "Billing for: <Month Year>" (or "All open
   unbilled"). Changing the selection refetches both endpoints with the same value. The
   preview query params are currently built at `customer-billing.tsx:313-330` — add
   `selectedMonth` there.

6. **Fix the right-panel detail UI** — The "Unbilled Work" badge already reads
   `totalUnbilledAmount` (`customer-billing.tsx:1199`) — now the cutoff-scoped full
   total. Update the subtext (`customer-billing.tsx:1206`) to show approved counts as
   "N items ready to bill" and a second line for pending counts ("P items pending
   approval — not yet billable"). `selectAllUnbilledItems` (`customer-billing.tsx:395`)
   and the invoice button disabled gate (`customer-billing.tsx:1214`) must continue
   acting on approved arrays only.

7. **Show all items in every tab, tagged** — In the "Unbilled" tab
   (`customer-billing.tsx:1255-1256` and its `TabsContent` body): render both approved
   and `pendingApproval*` arrays as one combined list. Approved rows get an enabled
   checkbox; unapproved rows get the checkbox disabled with a "pending approval" hint;
   undated rows show the "no work date" flag. The tab count becomes the total (approved
   + pending) for the selected month. Apply the same principle to the per-type tabs
   (Work Orders, Billing Sheets, WC Billings) so each tab's visible rows sum to its
   subtotal and subtotals sum to the headline. Build row lists and totals from the same
   Step-1 partition.

8. **Tests — cutoff + preview/detail invariants** — Synthesize a customer with: (a) an
   approved BS with `workDate` in the selected month, (b) an approved WO `completedAt`
   in an earlier never-billed month, (c) an approved WO `completedAt` in the month
   after the cutoff (future/next period), (d) an unapproved BS in the selected month,
   and (e) an unbilled item with a null work date. Assert: (a), (b), (d), (e) are
   included; (c) is excluded; `total` from `/api/customers/billing-preview` equals
   `totalUnbilledAmount` from `/api/customers/:id/billing` under the same
   `selectedMonth`, and both equal `approvedTotal + unapprovedTotal`. Add a
   default-resolution test: with no `selectedMonth`, the endpoint resolves to the
   previous calendar month. Add a timezone test: a record dated 10pm local on the last
   day of the month lands in that month, not the next.

9. **Verify Financial Pulse (no redesign)** — Confirm `routes/financial-pulse.ts`
   `computeUnbilledExposure` (`:357`) agrees with the Command Center All-open
   (`allOpenTotal`) figure on which customers carry unbilled dollars and how much.
   Financial Pulse is all-time exposure, so it reconciles to the no-cutoff view, not
   the month-scoped one. If the definition differs, note it in a code comment — do not
   silently change Financial Pulse math in this slice.
